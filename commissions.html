PORTRAIT COMMISSIONS

I accept a limited number of portrait commissions throughout the year. Each work is created with careful attention to observation, tone, and character, using either oil paint or charcoal depending on the nature of the image and intended outcome.

To inquire about a portrait commission please send an inquiry through the Commission Request Form, or via email with:

  • -A short description of your request
  • -Reference photographs
  • -Preferred size and medium


What I Offer

Portraits based on photographic reference

  • -Single subject
  • -Head-and-shoulders or simplified compositions
  • -Created in either oil paint or charcoal on paper/canvas

Each portrait is made with a strong emphasis on likeness, tonal structure, and atmospheric sensitivity.


Mediums

Oil Painting

  • Oil portraits are painted on gallery-wrapped fine linen canvas using only archival artist paints.
  • These works are designed as lasting pieces intended for display and collection.

Charcoal or Graphite Drawing

  • Charcoal or graphite portraits offer a more immediate, tonal interpretation.
  • They emphasize gesture, light, and form with a quieter, more direct visual language.


Sizes

Available standard formats:

  • -30 × 40 cm
  • -40 × 50 cm
  • -Custom sizes may be considered depending on availability.


Pricing

Pricing depends on size and medium. A general guide:

Charcoal Portraits

  • -30 × 40 cm: from 7,000 CZK
  • -40 × 50 cm: from 9,000 CZK

Oil portraits

  • -30 × 40 cm: from 13,000 CZK
  • -40 × 50 cm: from 17,000 CZK

A 50% deposit is required to secure a commission slot. The remaining balance is due prior to delivery.

Process

  1. 1. Inquiry. You send a request with reference photos and basic details through email.
  2. 2. Discussion. We confirm composition, medium, size, and timeline.
  3. 3. Deposit. A 50% deposit secures your place in the schedule. A compositional sketch will be approved before the work will begin on the canvas or archival paper.
  4. 4. Creation. I work from photographic reference, with updates if needed during key stages.
  5. 5. Completion. Final work is varnished (oil portraits) and carefully packaged for delivery.


Timeline

Portraits typically require several weeks depending on medium, drying time, and current schedule. Exact timing will be agreed upon before starting.
