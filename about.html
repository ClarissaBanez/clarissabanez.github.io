ABOUT

Clarissa Bañez (b. 1989, Manila, Philippines) is a contemporary artist based in Prague, working primarily in oil and graphite. Her work explores memory, mental tension, and the weight of domestic and emotional labor through a restrained visual language rooted in realism.

Clarissa paints directly from observation—a practice shaped by her experience of aphantasia, the inability to form mental images. Without internal visual recall, she turns to the external world to give form to intangible sensations: grief, pressure, devotion, tenderness. Her process is slow and deliberate, emphasizing archival materials and careful refinement.

Having originally trained as a medical doctor, and later navigating the intersecting worlds of motherhood, migration, and burnout, Clarissa’s art reflects a lifelong search for quietude and presence amid intensity. Through soft edges, sparse compositions, and suspended stillness, she asks: What remains in us after the noise fades?

She is currently completing an independent Artist Residency in Motherhood and developing her first portfolio of works for exhibition.
