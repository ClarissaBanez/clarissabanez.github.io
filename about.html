ABOUT

Clarissa Bañez is a Filipina visual artist living and working in Prague. Her paintings—executed in oil, graphite, and watercolor—merge quiet realism with emotional resonance. She captures stillness, not as emptiness, but as memory held in form: a fold of fabric, a shadow on a wall, the echo of a person just gone.

Originally trained as a medical doctor, Bañez brings a research-driven sensibility to her artmaking. Her attention to nuance—light, surface, transition—mirrors the diagnostic gaze: observant, exacting, and deeply curious. She is committed to archival materials and conservation-informed practice, treating each painting as a crafted, enduring object.

Living with aphantasia—the inability to form mental images—her imagination is conceptual rather than visual. Painting becomes a way to externalize thought and emotion, translating what cannot be pictured into form. Her process is slow, grounded in direct observation and quiet interpretation. Her work invites the viewer not to look harder, but to feel longer.
