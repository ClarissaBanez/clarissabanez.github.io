Artist Bio

Clarissa Bañez (b. 1989, Manila) is a Filipino artist based in Prague, Czech Republic working primarily with classical techniques and modern, minimally toxic materials. Her focus is on still life and portraiture, exploring themes of belonging, resilience, and cultural identity.

Clarissa's work is deeply influenced by her experiences as an immigrant and mother, reflecting the challenges and beauty of living in diverse cultures. She draws inspiration from classical masters such as Caravaggio, Rembrandt, and Luna, while overcoming the limitations of aphantasia through detailed planning and observational drawing.

Since relocating to Prague in 2022, Clarissa has pursued formal art education, blending traditional methods with contemporary themes in both oil and digital mediums. She aims to offer a visual refuge for multicultural families, celebrating the struggles and triumphs of living in a foreign land, while fostering a sense of belonging for those who feel displaced.

Artist Statement

As an artist, my work is driven by my deep curiosity and passion for learning. I explore classical techniques while embracing modern, minimally toxic materials in my oil paintings. My focus is on still life and portraiture, addressing themes of belonging, resilience, and cultural identity.

Through my art, I aim to provide a visual refuge for those navigating the complexities of a multicultural upbringing. As an immigrant, I've experienced both the challenges and beauty of living in different cultures, and my work reflects these experiences. I strive to inspire families to embrace their unique cultural identities and recognize the richness that comes from diverse environments.

My practice blends classical methods with contemporary themes, honoring tradition and sustainability while fostering a sense of belonging for those who feel displaced.
