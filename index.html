Artist Bio

Clarissa Bañez (b. 1989, Manila) is a Filipino artist based in Prague, working primarily in still life and portraiture. Blending classical techniques with modern, minimally toxic materials, her work explores themes of belonging, resilience, and cultural identity.

An immigrant and mother, she reflects on the shifting nature of home—not just as a place, but as something constantly shaped by relationships, heritage, and change. Inspired by Caravaggio, Rembrandt, and Luna, she navigates aphantasia through careful observation and deliberate strokes.

She continuously hones her craft, merging tradition with contemporary themes. Her work offers a quiet refuge—an invitation to reflect on identity, memory, and transformation within the ever-changing fabric of family life.

Artist Statement

My work is a meditation on belonging, woven from the daily rituals of family life and the evolving nature of cultural identity. Through still life and portraiture, I explore the way traditions are reshaped across generations, the quiet negotiations of identity within a multicultural home, and the balance between preservation and change.

As an immigrant mother, I witness my family’s culture unfolding in real time—merging inherited roots with new landscapes. Each painting becomes a reflection of this transformation, capturing both the comfort of the familiar and the beauty of adaptation.

By blending classical techniques with modern materials, I seek to honor the past while embracing the present—a practice that mirrors the way we build, redefine, and carry home with us wherever we go.
